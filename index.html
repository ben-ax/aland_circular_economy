The construction sector is traditionally a massive producer of waste. Åland is tackling this through regional cooperation and rethinking building materials as permanent assets.

  • Waste to Value Project (2025–2028): An EU-funded initiative designed to systematically reduce virgin material use.
  • The Jomala Hub: Led by Emmaus Åland, this new facility serves as a massive drop-off and marketplace for leftover building materials.
  • Baltic Sea Impact: The policy recommendations developed here will be shared to influence circular practices across the entire Baltic Sea region.

Tourism is vital to Åland, but it requires significant resources. The hospitality sector is combating this through stringent eco-certifications and innovative financial models.

  • Smart Philanthropy: Ålandhotels donates €1 to the Keep the Archipelago Tidy Association for every skipped daily cleaning, generating >€10,000 annually.
  • Rigorous Eco-Certifications: Major commercial properties hold recognized labels like the Nordic Swan ecolabel and Green Key.
  • Heritage Reuse (Hotel Svala): An abandoned 1930s hospital in Kumlinge was entirely repurposed into a modern hotel using a strict low-waste ethos.

A true circular economy requires that consumer goods remain in circulation. Åland's service sector is proving that "used" is simply a state of transition.

  • Skill Building Workshops: The organization hosts active repair and reuse workshops, teaching locals the skills needed to fix clothing and refurbish furniture.
  • Social Circularity: These service centers provide crucial work integration, combining environmental sustainability with social sustainability.
